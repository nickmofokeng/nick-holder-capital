## Post copy

Markets reward discipline more consistently than they reward opinion.

At Nick-Holder Capital Group, our approach to trading GER40 is built on a defined, rules-based sequence rather than discretionary calls. Every trade is evaluated against the same four conditions, in the same order, every time:

1. A liquidity sweep — price takes out a prior high or low
2. Displacement — a decisive, impulsive move away from that level
3. A return to the resulting fair value gap
4. A defined confirmation signal before entry

No step is skipped. No exception is made because a setup "feels right."

That same discipline extends to risk. Position sizing, the number of trades taken in a day, and the point at which trading pauses for the week are all fixed in advance — not decided in the moment.

We think of this as infrastructure, not intuition: a documented process that can be reviewed, audited, and repeated, independent of any single day's market noise or any single day's conviction.

Systematic trading isn't about predicting where the market goes next. It's about having a process disciplined enough that prediction isn't required.
